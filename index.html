About California Redwoods

California redwoods are among the tallest trees on Earth, with some reaching heights of over 350 feet. These ancient giants have stood for thousands of years, creating cathedral-like groves that inspire awe and wonder.

The coast redwood (Sequoia sempervirens) thrives in the foggy coastal regions of Northern California, where the mild climate and consistent moisture create perfect growing conditions.

Conservation Efforts

Protecting these ancient forests is crucial for future generations. Through dedicated conservation efforts, we work to preserve the remaining old-growth redwood forests and restore damaged ecosystems.

  • Habitat restoration programs
  • Research and monitoring
  • Education and awareness
